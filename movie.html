Prisoners


Synopsis

Prisoners is a thriller starring Hugh Jackman and Jake Gyllenhaal. When the daughters of two families go missing on Thanksgiving day, Kelly Dover (Hugh Jackman) does whatever it takes to find them. A devout Christian, Dover delivers Hell to Alex Jones (Paul Dano), the accused abductor and troubled adult that has the mind of a ten-year old.

The Actors

Prisoners has a star studded cast, including the stoic Detective Loki as played by Jake Gyllenhaal, The high octane and improvising Kelly Dover played by Hugh Jackman, and the unsettling disposition of Alex Jones played by Paul Dano.

Hugh Jackman

The performance of Hugh Jackman in this movie is spectacular, I have watched every X-Men and Wolverine movie to date, and I am confident in saying this is his best acting performance. What makes his role as Kelly Dover work so well is his ability to make people resonate with his emotions in the movie. For example, Take the scene in which he is spying on Alex Jones as he is walking his dog late in the night. As Jones is walking the dog, he pauses by the sidewalk, and randomly begins to hang the dog by its leash with a smile on his face. When Dover sees this happen, he immediately reaches in his coat for his pistol, with a face of absolute disgust. When watching this with friends and family who have never seen the movie, they exclaim and jump out of their seats. They are pissed off just like Dover, and seeing him later on torturing Jones seems more like justice than flat out senseless violence.
